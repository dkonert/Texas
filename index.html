Before joining the United States in 1845, the territory of Texas had a rich and varied history. From 1519 until 1821, Texas was part of the Spanish Empire. From 1821 to 1836, it was a province of Mexico. The short-lived Republic of Texas was then formed, before the region became the 28th state in the U.S. It the second largest state with a land mass of 268,597 square miles. Texas is roughly 7% of the total land size of the U.S.

Texas is known for its diverse landscapes ranging from deserts to forests, and is famous for its BBQ and cowboy culture. It is home to major cities like Austin, Houston, and Dallas. Texas plays an important role in our nation's economy with its booming oil industry, tech hubs, and agricultural output. "Everything is bigger in Texas" — from the landscapes to the spirit!

Weather 10

Texas experiences long and hot summers with temperatures often exceeding 100°F. Its rainy season mainly occurs in the spring (March-May) and occasionally in early fall (September-October). Spring also brings severe weather, including thunderstorms, tornadoes, and hail, while coastal areas are at risk of hurricanes, particularly in late summer and fall. Winters are generally mild, though temperatures can vary by up to 20 degrees between the northern and southern regions. Snow is rare, but freezing and ice are more common in the northern areas, typically short-lived.
